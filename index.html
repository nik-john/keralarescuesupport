The Keralarescue team is running a real-time 24x7 chat helpline This helpine provides key information to people in need and makes requests for emergency rescues.

Join as a Chat Volunteer

  1. Download Slack and join keralarescue on Android, iOS or Web.

  2. Join the #chatsupport channel on Slack

  3. Download the Tawk App

  4. Fill the Google Sheet made by Nabil here

  5. You will receive an email with the subject You have been invited to join tawk...’

  6. In case of delays, please send a mesaage to @Benoy, @Nabil Nalakath, or @pj_paul on Slack

    Before your chat

  • Do not start chatting with callers right away. First, observe an experienced volunteer in action. You can do this by clicking on an ongoing chat. Be careful not to join the chat, but just to observe.
  • Wait for the user to send the first message. Do not initiate the chat.
  • Talk politely and try to collect necessary information from the visitors as quickly as possible.
  • Please remember that people may be worried and tensed, so please keep these in mind when talking to them. You should use whatever language(s) the person chatting is convenient.
  • Only one person may chat with a user at a time. If you would like to enter an already engaged chat, please use the Whisper tab to speak.

    During your Chat

  • Try and obtain a
    1. Contact number
    2. Name of the person needing help
    3. Location (with Cordinates if possible)
  • Use the details obtained in the step above to confirm that this request is not already present in our database.
    1. Search for the contact number (or other details) here
    2. You may also provide details from the map of resources here here
    3. Details on relief camps in districts are here (link under development) here
  • If the person provides all details, don’t ask them again to report directly on Keralarescue Instead, you are to file it as shown below.

    After your Chat

  • If the chat requires immediate attention, generate a ticket. The menu to generate a ticket is available in the bottom right corner of the chat window. You can also use this menu to tag the chat as well. The following tags are available
    • rescue
    • info
    • safe_area
  • Re-confirm that the request is not already present in our dataset here

  • Try to contact the number provided from the chat, confirm and file the details here with confirmed details.
  • Post requests for urgent rescue in the #rescue_requests Slack channel. Post this using the \todo command. Example: /todo Rescue Mr ABC from [lat-long] contact 998y8488
  • After a chat has ended Use the X icon on the top right to close a chat.
